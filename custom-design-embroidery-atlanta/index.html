Embroidery Design Considerations

Great embroidery design requires understanding certain technical realities:

Detail Limitations

Embroidery thread has physical thickness—extremely fine lines and tiny text don't translate well. We advise on minimum sizes and detail levels for clear results.

Color Simplification

Unlike printing, embroidery can't reproduce gradients or photographic images. We simplify color palettes and create thread-appropriate designs.

Size Matters

A design that works at 4" may need modification at 2". We create size-specific versions to ensure your design looks great at every application size.

Fabric Considerations

Different fabrics require different approaches. Stretchy fabrics, thick canvas, delicate silks—we adjust designs and digitizing for optimal results on your chosen material.

File Formats We Accept

We can work with virtually any file type—but some are better than others:

Ideal (Vector)

  • AI - Adobe Illustrator
  • EPS - Encapsulated PostScript
  • PDF - Portable Document Format
  • SVG - Scalable Vector Graphics

Vector files scale infinitely without quality loss. These are ideal for logo work and produce the best embroidery results.

Acceptable (Raster)

  • PNG - Portable Network Graphics
  • JPG - JPEG images
  • TIFF - Tagged Image File Format
  • PSD - Photoshop Document

High-resolution raster files (300 DPI minimum) can work well. Lower resolution may require recreation or cause quality issues.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you work from a rough sketch or idea?

Absolutely! Some of our best work started as napkin sketches or verbal descriptions. Share whatever you have—a rough drawing, reference images, or just words describing your vision. We'll translate it into professional design.

How many revision rounds are included?

Standard projects include 2-3 revision rounds. Most clients find their perfect design within these rounds. Additional revisions are available if needed at reasonable hourly rates.

Will you match our existing brand colors?

Yes! Send us your Pantone codes or brand guidelines. We match thread colors as closely as possible and document them for all future orders. We'll show you thread samples before production.

How long does the design process take?

Simple optimization: 2-3 business days. New design creation: 5-10 business days depending on complexity. Rush service available for urgent projects—just let us know your deadline.

What if my logo has complex gradients or photographic elements?

Embroidery can't reproduce gradients or photographs directly. We'll advise on simplification options that maintain your brand identity while working within embroidery's capabilities. Often, simplified versions look even better in thread.

Do I own the final design?

Yes! Any original artwork created for you is yours to own and use however you wish. We provide vector files upon request for use in printing, web, and other applications.

Can you create designs for items you won't embroider?

Our primary focus is embroidery-ready design. While we can create logos suitable for multiple applications, we're not a general graphic design studio. Our expertise is in designs that stitch beautifully.

What's included in design pricing?

Design quotes include consultation, concept development, revision rounds, and digitizing for embroidery. Embroidery production is quoted separately. Design fees are often credited toward larger orders.

Can you design patches?

Yes! Patch design requires specific considerations including border treatment, edge finishing, and size specifications. We create patch-ready designs optimized for embroidered patch production.

How do I provide feedback on designs?

We share designs via email with clear images. Provide feedback in writing—be as specific as possible. Mark-ups, sketches, and detailed descriptions all help us refine the design to your vision.
